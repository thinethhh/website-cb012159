Hikkaduwa turtle hatchery
Southern province, Galle

Located north of Hikkaduwa, the Sea Turtle Hatchery and Rescue Centre serves as a vital conservation hub for endangered turtle species. Among the numerous turtle hatcheries along the southern coast of the island, this center stands out. Originally established as a community initiative to safeguard the region's biodiversity, it has evolved into a comprehensive facility with dedicated teams tirelessly studying, nurturing, and safeguarding various turtle species. Committed to their mission, the center operates continuously, offering round-the-clock care and protection. It plays a crucial role in the ongoing efforts to preserve these magnificent creatures, contributing to the overall conservation of marine life.

CONVERSATION EFFORTS


  • The hatchery cares for all the five species of turtles that are in Sri Lanka.
  • State-of-the-art facilities include water tanks for newborn and injured adult turtles.
  • Turtles lay their eggs in distinct mounds of sand, closely monitored for hatching.
  • Teams study and monitor turtle eggs for development and survival.
  • The center aids in conservation, providing a safe environment for nesting and hatching.
  • Injured adult turtles are rescued, treated, and released back into the wild.
